Storyline

The Turtles continue to live in the shadows and no one knows they were the ones who took down Shredder. And Vernon is the one everyone thinks is the one who took Shredder down. April O'Neill does some snooping and learns a scientist named, Baxter Stockman is working for Shredder. He plans to break him out while he's being transported. April tells the turtles who try to stop it but can't. Stockman tries to teleport Shredder but he some how ends up in another dimension and meets a warlord named Krang who instructs Shredder to assemble a teleportation device he sent to Earth a long time ago. He gives Shredder some mutagen which he uses to transform two criminals who were also in the transport with him, Rock Steady and Bebop into mutants. And they set out to find the device. April saw the transformation while investigating Stockman, She would take the mutagen and she would be chased by Shredder's minions, the Foot Clan. User Reviews

 
True to the franchise
9 June 2016 | by usersx – See all my reviews

When i was a kid i loved these turtles and i really appreciate the effort people put into reviving them on the big screen. People or critics always tend to find everything that is bad in a movie, but there's nothing bad in this one. Acting is solid, plot is good, motion capture is really cool (chech the feature video). I go to movies because i want to enjoy them, not because i want to judge them. If it sucks then I wont try to sell this movie to my friends, but if its good then i do. This movie is fun. You can go watch it with your kids or just sit and slide back to see your childhood heroes in a slightly new cgi coat. Its worth the watch, definitely 10 stars for me. Go and see this movie, we want a third one.

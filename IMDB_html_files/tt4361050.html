Ouija: Origin of Evil
21 October 2016 | by Argemaluco (Argentina) – See all my reviews

I think Ouija: Origin of Evil is superior to its predecessor, Ouija, but I still didn't find it very satisfactory. What hurts me the most is that Ouija: Origin of Evil starts pretty well, and it eventually degenerates into chaos and confusion which demerit its pros. To start with, we can compare the premise of this prequel with the one of the original Ouija: in the first film, a group of friends plays with an ouija and unleashes the rage of a vindictive ghost; the lack of effort and creativity in setting up the premise is clearly noticed. On the opposite, Ouija: Origin of Evil presents a much more interesting situation: a dishonest, but not malicious, "psychic" has sincere intentions to relieve the sorrow of her customers with false "readings" she organizes with the help of her daughters, employing classic tricks of spiritism which were already employed in 19th century; their introduction to the ouija is only with "theatrical" purposes, in order to make the experiences of the customers more convincing, until strange things start to happen. Unfortunately, as I previously mentioned, Ouija: Origin of Evil eventually loses cohesion while introducing arbitrary manifestations which feel forced and excessive, until getting to a confusing ending. Nevertheless, Elizabeth Reaser, Annalise Basso, Lulu Wilson and Henry Thomas feel credible and enthusiastic in their roles. In conclusion, after a pretty good start, Ouija: Origin of Evil ends up disappointing for not being able to keep the same narrative discipline afterwards. However, it kept me moderately entertained, and I think I can give it a slight recommendation. To sum up, Ouija: Origin of Evil offers a tolerable horror dose to occupy our time, but it's mediocre and definitely inferior to many films which are much more suitable to enjoy during the Halloween month.

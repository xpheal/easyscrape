Storyline

The Governor convinces his new community that it is in their best interests to take over the prison and if necessary, use force to do so. Not everyone is comfortable with that approach - Lilly and Meghan stay behind, with tragic consequences. The Governor and his crew appear at the prison and has two bargaining chips with him - Herschel and Michonne are his prisoners. He tells Rick to be out by sundown. Rick for his part tries to reason with him but the die is cast. In the ensuing battle, many - from both sides - are killed. Written by garykmcd

User Reviews

 
This episode made me quit watching the show.
29 April 2014 | by hellishfiend (United States) – See all my reviews

This episode contains a random pointless reverse deus ex tragedy that really rubbed me the wrong way. I refuse to continue watching now that they are resorting to contrived tragedies that serve no purpose but to callously traumatize the viewer and the characters in the show. The first two seasons contained wonderful character development and showed how both strong and weak people deal with adversity. The show no longer contains any of the elements that drew me to it in the first place. Season 3 was downhill as they introduced too many new characters and left them underdeveloped, and now season 4 has taken another turn for the worse. Goodbye Walking Dead, thanks for wasting my time.
